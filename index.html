Sustainable Water Management and Safe Sanitation

Water is essential for life, yet billions of people around the world lack access to safe and affordable drinking water and sanitation. This is not only a health issue, but also a barrier to poverty reduction, food security, peace, human rights, and education. By managing our water sustainably, we can also improve food and energy production, create jobs, and combat climate change. Let's work together to ensure that everyone has access to clean water and sanitation by 2030.

Why It Matters

Water is a vital resource for all life on Earth. It is essential for agriculture, industry, and energy production, as well as for human health and well-being. However, water scarcity, pollution, and climate change threaten this vital resource. We need to take action to ensure that we manage our water sustainably and equitably, so that everyone has access to safe and affordable drinking water and sanitation.

Things To Do

  • Keep governments accountable for their commitments to sustainable water management and access to safe water and sanitation.
  • Invest in water research and development to find innovative solutions to water challenges.
  • Promote water conservation and efficiency in all sectors.
  • Include diverse voices in water resources governance, especially those of women, indigenous peoples, and marginalized communities.

The Eight Targets

  • Ensure universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water.
  • Achieve access to adequate and equitable sanitation and hygiene for all.
  • Improve water quality by reducing pollution and increasing recycling and safe reuse.
  • Ensure sustainable withdrawals and supply of freshwater resources.
  • Increase water use efficiency across all sectors.
  • Implement integrated water resources management at all levels.
  • Protect and restore water-related ecosystems.
  • Increase cooperation and coordination on water management at all levels.
